Borobudur

Borobudur Temple (Javanese: ꦕꦟ꧀ꦝꦶꦧꦫꦧꦸꦝꦸꦂ, translit. Candhi Båråbudhur) is a Buddhist temple located in Borobudur, Magelang, Central Java, Indonesia. This temple is located approximately 100 km southwest of Semarang, 86 km west of Surakarta, and 40 km northwest of Yogyakarta. This temple with many stupas was built by Mahayana Buddhists around the 800s AD during the reign of the Syailendra dynasty. Borobudur is the largest Buddhist temple or shrine in the world, as well as one of the largest Buddhist monuments in the world.
